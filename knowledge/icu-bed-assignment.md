# ICU bed assignment (operational guidance)

When demand exceeds ICU capacity, decisions must align with documented triage rules and real-time bed availability. The next bed generally goes to the highest-acuity patient on the waitlist who is clinically appropriate for an ICU-level bed.

Overflow to step-down or general beds may be allowed only when policy permits overflow assignment and the patient’s needs match the bed type. Never assign a patient to a bed type that cannot meet their monitoring or intervention requirements.

Document the rationale for non-obvious decisions (e.g., deferring a high-priority case due to infection control or bed type mismatch).
