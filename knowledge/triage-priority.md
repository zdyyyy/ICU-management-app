# Triage and priority levels

Priority reflects clinical urgency for ICU admission, not arrival order. CRITICAL typically indicates immediate life-threatening instability or need for intensive monitoring and intervention. HIGH indicates serious risk of deterioration. MEDIUM and LOW reflect lower immediate risk but may still warrant admission when capacity allows.

When comparing two patients, prefer the one with higher assigned priority unless contraindicated (e.g., infection isolation requirements, incompatible bed type, or patient refusal).

Communicate changes in priority clearly to bedside staff and bed management so the waitlist order stays defensible and auditable.
